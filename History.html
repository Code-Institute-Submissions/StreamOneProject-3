Timeline Events of the Pencil

1300BC

During Roman times, people used a thin pointed metal rod called a stylus to write with. They wrote on a soft wax tablet using the sharp end. To use the tablet again, or rub out a mistake you smoothed the wax over with the blunt end. For important letters the Romans used a metal pen dipped in ink, which were wrote on thin pieces of wood or on specially prepared animal skins.

1795

A swiss man called Conrad Gressner during the 15th Century worked as a scientist studying Biology, recorded the earliest description of a pencil. It was described in his wrtitten work on fossils, the author depicting "a wooden tube holding a piece of graphite".

1812

Willam Munroe manufatured the first American lead pencils in 1812. He made the graphite formula for his pencils in secret, with only the help of his wife. The business was quite successful. By 1814 he had made nearly 175,000 pencils, which sold for about six thousand dollars, a very large amount of money at the time. When the war with England ended there were better pencils available from Europe and his profits were erased by these better imports.

1834

There is no exact information available on the invention of the coloured pencil, but Staedtler is found in corresponding historical documents.
In 1834, one year before the foundation of the company, Johann Sebastian Staedtler had already invented a coloured oil pastel pencil that could be sharpened to a point just like a pencil, making it one of the first coloured pencils ever

1938

Production of colored pencils for water art purposes started in early 20th century. Berol started making its water color pencils in 1938. Few manufacturers claim that they invented the first watercolor pencil.

1957

The first graphic tablet resembling modern day tablets and used for handwriting recognition by a computer was the Stylator in 1957. Digitizers were popularized in the mid-1970s and early 1980s by the commercial success of the ID (Intelligent Digitizer).The first home computer graphic tablet was the KoalaPad, though originally designed for the Apple II. The later millenia graphic pads and stylus are used by graphic designers for website design.
